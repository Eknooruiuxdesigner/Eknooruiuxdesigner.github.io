About Me

Hey there! I’m Eknoor Kaur, a UI/UX Designer with an artist’s soul and a thinker’s heart. My design journey didn’t start on a screen — it began with a pencil.

As a child, I was always sketching — people, places, faces. I still remember the first time I drew a portrait that actually looked like someone I knew. That feeling of capturing emotion on paper stuck with me, and even now, I sketch portraits in my free time — not just for practice, but to stay connected to the purest form of expression.

I’ve always been someone who looks closely at the world around me. Whether I’m walking through a store, scrolling an app, or sipping coffee at a café — I can’t help but notice how things are designed. From signboards to spacing between elements, design is everywhere, and I love dissecting the why behind each choice.

From Pencil to Prototypes

I transitioned into digital design with a solid base in storytelling and empathy. I joined Tatras Data Services Pvt. Ltd., where I spent over 3 years designing enterprise tools, dashboards, and customer-facing products. That experience sharpened my skills in UX research, responsive design, and collaborative problem-solving in fast-paced environments.

Alongside, I’ve been working as a freelance designer, helping companies and startups turn ideas into intuitive, beautiful, and purposeful designs. From wireframes to final UI, I help bring digital products to life.

“Whether it’s a personal sketch or a client dashboard,
I design with feeling, structure, and purpose.”

Forever Learning, Forever Growing

I’m a firm believer that great designers never stop learning. I also read design books to dig deeper into why users behave the way they do — not just how to design, but how to design well.

My Approach to Design

I believe that great design is quiet, thoughtful, and deeply human. It doesn’t scream. It speaks — in clear flows, visual balance, and tiny delightful moments.
Here’s how I approach every project:

  • Sketch, explore, empathize

  • Think like the use

  • Focus on clarity and emotion

  • Iterate, test, and improve

And yes — I still begin many of my ideas on paper.
